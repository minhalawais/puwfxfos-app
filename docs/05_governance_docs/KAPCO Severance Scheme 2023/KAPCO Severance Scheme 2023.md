Background: The Company’s Power Purchase Agreement dated June 27, 1996 (as amended, from
time to time) (“PPA”) expired on October 24, 2022. Prior to, and since the expiry of the PPA, the
Company has been engaged with the relevant ministry, regulator and power purchaser for the
renewal/extension of the PPA. To this end, tariff applications were submitted before the National
Electric Power Regulatory Authority (“NEPRA”). On August 4, 2022, NEPRA issued a provisional
tariff for a total capacity of 500 MW (in line with approved the Indicative Generation Capacity
Expansion Plan (IGCEP) 2022-31) of Block 1 comprising GT-13, GT-14 & STG-15 and Block 2
comprising GT-1 & STG-9 on ‘take and pay’ basis. A public hearing of the main tariff petition was
held on October 3, 2023. For clarity, a list of the Blocks and business units that do not form part


of the 500 MW total capacity and are required to be closed is attached as Annex A.


You will appreciate that, unlike other companies, our Board of Directors has extended full support
to the current human resource structure and benefits be maintained in an amicable and
sustainable manner, The Board of Directors and Management equally appreciates your patience
and continued support in these challenging times. Unfortunately, the situation vis-a-vis the
Company's future operations and the present human resources is no longer sustainable. Thus,
certain decisions have to be made in the best interest of the Company and its stakeholders
(including Company employees); and so it has been decided to realign/right size the Company's
human resource across all sections of the Company in accordance with the new indicative
operating regime of the Company. As a first step in this direction, a selective voluntary severance


scheme (“Severance Scheme”) is offered to employees on the terms and conditions appearing


hereunder. Wh

Employees who wish to be considered for severance in accordance with the Severance Scheme


should fill in the Registration for Severance Scheme Form (“Registration Form”) attached as


Annex B and submit the same with the Senior Manager HR&CS within the given timeline.


Terms and Conditions: The terms and conditions of the Severance Scheme are as follows:


i.


Employees whose request for participation in the Severance Scheme is accepted by the
Company will be entitled to twelve (12) monthly Gross Salaries (as defined in HR Manual)
("Severance Entitlement”). However, in case of employees whose remaining service is
less than twelve (12) months, their Severance Entitlement will be equal to Gross Salaries


for remaining service with the Company.


Employees to submit their completed Registration Form not later than 5,30 pm on
January 02, 2024 (“Registration Date”) under the Severance Scheme. The Company shall
not accept any submission for Severance Entitlement after the expiry of the Registration


Date; and the Severance Scheme shall stand withdrawn.


An employee who is on the Company's pension scheme may apply for the Severance
Entitlement; and concurrently, such employee shall also apply for early retirement,


subject to and in accordance with the Company's HR Manual.


An employee who is entitled to post-retirement medical and electricity benefits may also


apply for lump-sum payment in lieu of these benefits.


An employee who is entitled to LPR/frozen leave shall also apply for encashment in


accordance with the Company's HR Manual.


Once the completed Registration Form is submitted, it cannot be withdrawn.
